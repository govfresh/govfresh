<p>A policy hackathon will be held in San Francisco on September 24 to "tackle problems brought by cities from the U.S. and Europe." The event is part of <a href="https://sec2sv.com/">Startup Europe Comes to Silicon Valley</a>.</p>
<!-- /paragraph -->

<!-- paragraph -->
<p>Details below:</p>
<!-- /paragraph -->

<!-- quote -->
<blockquote class="wp-block-quote"><p><strong>What</strong>: Policy Hackathon addressing challenges brought by cities</p><p><strong>Who</strong>: Government leaders, including from Spain, Italy and Norway, bring real challenges.  Policy hackers will include entrepreneurs from Europe and the United States, investors, policy makers, and academics.</p><p><strong>When</strong>: Tuesday afternoon, September 24, followed by a reception and light dinner.</p><p><strong>Where</strong>: Headquarters of Mind the Bridge, 450 Townsend St, San Francisco, CA</p><p><strong>Roles</strong>: government representatives with a specific challenge to be hacked; policy hackers to develop and present possible responses to the challenge; judges to determine the best response.</p><p>The Policy Hack brings together experts from across the startup ecosystem to design solutions to policy challenges faced by local governments and is part of <a rel="noreferrer noopener" href="https://sec2sv.com/" target="_blank">Startup Europe Comes to Silicon Valley</a>. </p><p>Several teams composed by entrepreneurs, investors, corporate executives, academics, and policy makers will be discussing challenges in these areas.</p><p>After a couple of hours of brainstorming (“hacking”) within each team, the solutions are then pitched to a panel of judges, who select the most convincing one.</p></blockquote>
<!-- /quote -->
